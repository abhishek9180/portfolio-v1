# Content Style Guide

This document defines how portfolio content should be written.

It does **not** define factual information.
Facts always come from `profile.md`.

---

# Overall Tone

Professional.

Confident.

Approachable.

Modern.

Authentic.

Avoid sounding corporate or AI-generated.

---

# Writing Perspective

Use first person where appropriate.

Examples:

"I enjoy building scalable frontend systems."

"I've led engineering initiatives..."

Avoid awkward third-person biography unless the template explicitly requires it.

---

# Professional Positioning

Present the portfolio as an experienced software engineer who:

- owns technical decisions
- solves business problems
- leads engineering initiatives
- mentors developers
- delivers measurable impact

Avoid presenting yourself as a freelancer unless stated in profile.md.

---

# Writing Principles

Prefer concise writing.

Every sentence should provide value.

Remove unnecessary filler.

Avoid repeating technologies throughout multiple sections.

Prioritize readability over keyword stuffing.

---

# Achievement Style

Focus on outcomes instead of responsibilities.

Preferred:

✔ Reduced deployment time by 60%.

✔ Led migration to Next.js App Router.

✔ Built reusable design system adopted across teams.

Avoid:

✘ Responsible for React development.

✘ Worked on frontend.

✘ Participated in meetings.

---

# Technical Content

Mention technologies only when they provide context.

Do not list technologies inside paragraphs unless relevant.

Technology stacks belong in dedicated sections whenever possible.

---

# Numbers

Whenever profile.md contains measurable results:

- percentages
- revenue
- latency improvements
- team sizes
- scale
- users

always preserve them.

Never invent metrics.

---

# Hero Section

The hero should immediately communicate:

- seniority
- specialization
- value proposition

Avoid generic headlines like:

"Full Stack Developer"

Prefer concise positioning.

Example style:

Senior Frontend Engineer building scalable web applications with modern frontend architecture and AI-powered solutions.

---

# About Section

Should answer:

Who are you?

What problems do you solve?

What kind of engineer are you?

Avoid chronological life stories.

---

# Experience

Each experience entry should emphasize:

- ownership
- architecture
- technical leadership
- business impact
- measurable achievements

Do not rewrite experience as a job description.

---

# Projects

Describe projects using:

Problem

Solution

Impact

Technologies

Avoid listing features without explaining why they matter.

---

# Skills

Organize skills into logical categories.

Avoid long comma-separated lists.

---

# Language

Use active voice.

Examples:

Designed

Built

Led

Optimized

Scaled

Delivered

Mentored

Improved

Avoid passive voice whenever possible.

---

# AI Writing Restrictions

Do not use phrases such as:

- passionate developer
- coding enthusiast
- highly motivated
- results-driven professional
- think outside the box
- leverage
- cutting-edge
- world-class
- rockstar
- ninja
- guru

Avoid obvious AI wording such as:

"With a passion for..."

"I thrive in..."

"I am dedicated to..."

---

# Consistency

Maintain a consistent writing style across every section.

Every page should feel like it was written by the same person.
